The Boring Score.

Our signature framework for rating every boring business across five critical criteria.

Criterion 01

Effort

How many hours per week does this business demand from the owner? Can it run semi-passively, or have you just bought yourself a 60-hour-a-week job? We rate from 1 (full-time grind) to 5 (near-passive).

Criterion 02

Capital Required

What's the real startup cost? Not the optimistic version — the actual number including real estate, equipment, permits, working capital, and the inevitable surprises. Rated 1 (millions) to 5 (under $10k).

Criterion 03

Income Ceiling

What's the realistic maximum annual income from a single operation? Can you scale to multiple locations? Is this a $50k business or a $500k business? Rated 1 (capped low) to 5 (sky-high).

Criterion 04

Recession Resistance

When the economy contracts, does this business survive or collapse? Are customers cutting this expense first, or is demand inelastic? Rated 1 (first to die) to 5 (recession-proof).

Criterion 05

Exit Potential

Can you sell this business? Is there an active market of buyers? Do private equity firms actively acquire in this space? Rated 1 (unsellable) to 5 (PE targets this category).

The Formula

Final Score

The Boring Score is the unweighted average of all five criteria. A score of 4.0+ means the business is "Highly Boring, Highly Profitable." A score below 2.5 means proceed with extreme caution.
