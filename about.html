The Manifesto.

Who writes this, why boring beats sexy, and what we're trying to do.

Why This Exists

For too long, the business world has been obsessed with unicorns, massive venture rounds, and explosive software growth. Meanwhile, the actual bedrock of the economy — the businesses that silently generate massive cash flow month after month — are completely ignored by media, by writers, by everyone.

A guy named Dennis in Fresno owns four car washes, works 20 hours a week, and clears $340,000 a year. Nobody writes about Dennis. This newsletter is about Dennis.

What We Believe

Boring businesses are beautiful. They're recession-resistant, cash-flowing, and acquirable. They don't need venture capital. They don't need press coverage. They don't need a Series A. They need an operator who shows up, understands the numbers, and runs a tight ship.

We believe the best businesses in the world are the ones most people drive past without thinking about. Car washes. Storage units. Laundromats. Parking lots. Vending routes. These are the businesses that build real, generational wealth.

How We Work

Every week, we pick one boring business and tear it apart. We talk to operators, collect real financial data, assess the risks, and present it all in an honest, no-fluff format. We rate every business using our signature Boring Score framework — effort, capital, income ceiling, recession resistance, and exit potential.

We don't take money from the businesses we cover. We don't run sponsored content disguised as analysis. The newsletter is free, the knowledge is the product, and our readers are the audience — not the commodity.

"Nobody covers the quiet economy. We do."

— BoringMoney Founding Team
